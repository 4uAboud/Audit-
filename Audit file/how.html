How UX Audit Pro works

A professional-grade evaluation process, powered by AI and grounded in established UX research methodology.

The process

Four steps to a complete audit.

01

Upload your inputs

Provide the raw material for analysis. The more context you give, the more precise and relevant the findings will be.

  • Screenshots of your UI (PNG, JPG, WebP)
  • Reference files: PDFs, design specs, Figma exports
  • Product description and target user profile
  • Business context and evaluation focus
02

Configure your evaluation

Choose exactly what gets evaluated. Toggle on the heuristics and accessibility checks that matter for your product.

  • Select from Nielsen's 10 Heuristics
  • Choose WCAG accessibility checks
  • Add competitor products for comparison
  • Pick benchmarking dimensions
03

AI runs the analysis

The AI evaluates your UI against every selected criterion simultaneously — the same analysis a UX researcher would conduct, delivered in under 2 minutes.

  • Visual analysis of your screenshots
  • Heuristic scoring with severity levels
  • WCAG accessibility pass/warn/fail
  • Competitive comparison table
04

Get your report

Review findings inline, then export in the format you need. Reports are structured for both design teams and executive stakeholders.

  • Download as HTML report
  • Copy as Markdown for docs
  • Print or export as PDF
  • Generate extended narrative reports
Evaluation methods

What gets evaluated.

Heuristic evaluation — Nielsen's 10

Jakob Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics are the gold standard for UX evaluation. Each heuristic is scored 0–10 and given a severity rating from 0 (no issue) to 4 (critical). Findings include specific issues observed in your UI and concrete, actionable recommendations.

H1Visibility of system status
H2Match with real world
H3User control & freedom
H4Consistency & standards
H5Error prevention
H6Recognition over recall
H7Flexibility & efficiency
H8Aesthetic & minimal design
H9Error recognition & recovery
H10Help & documentation

Accessibility audit — WCAG 2.1

Evaluated across the four WCAG principles (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust) plus specific checks for color contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader support, focus indicators, alt text, and motion sensitivity. Each check returns pass, warning, or fail — with a WCAG reference and remediation guidance.

UX benchmarking

Your product is scored across up to 10 UX dimensions: learnability, efficiency, memorability, error rate, satisfaction, visual clarity, navigation, accessibility, onboarding, and performance feel. Scores are presented as a comparison table with bar charts for instant readability.

Competitive analysis

Add any competitors by name and the AI will compare your product against them side-by-side on every selected benchmarking dimension. Strengths and weaknesses are surfaced for each product, with strategic recommendations on where to focus to gain competitive advantage.

FAQ

Common questions.

Do I need to upload screenshots? ▾
Screenshots are strongly recommended — they allow the AI to visually analyse your UI and give much more specific findings. Without screenshots, the audit will rely on your description alone, which produces more general recommendations. For best results, upload screenshots of your most important flows.
How accurate are the AI findings? ▾
The AI provides expert-level heuristic analysis grounded in established UX research methodology. It performs well for identifying visual, structural, and interaction-level issues. For accessibility, it evaluates based on what's visible in screenshots — a full automated WCAG scan with real browser tooling will catch additional code-level issues. We recommend using this tool as a starting point and complement to (not replacement for) user research.
How long does an audit take? ▾
Typically 30–90 seconds depending on the number of screenshots, heuristics selected, and whether competitive benchmarking is included. A full 4-method audit with multiple competitors takes around 60–90 seconds.
What file formats can I upload? ▾
Screenshots: PNG, JPG, WebP — any standard image format. Reference files: PDF, TXT, Markdown, CSV. Figma and Sketch exports are accepted as reference files (export your frames as PDF or images for best results).
How do I export the results? ▾
After the analysis completes, you can download a fully formatted HTML report (open in any browser), copy results as Markdown for Notion/Confluence, or use your browser's print dialog to export as PDF. All formats include every finding, score, benchmark, and recommendation from the analysis.
Can I run audits on multiple screens or flows? ▾
Yes — upload as many screenshots as you need. The AI will analyse all of them together as a cohesive product experience. For best results, upload screenshots that represent the key flow you want to evaluate (e.g. all screens in a checkout flow, or all states of an onboarding sequence).
Is my data stored or used for training? ▾
Uploads are sent directly to the AI API for analysis and are not stored on our servers. The tool runs entirely in your browser — screenshots are read locally and sent only to the AI model for the duration of the analysis call. Check the AI provider's data handling policy for full details.
